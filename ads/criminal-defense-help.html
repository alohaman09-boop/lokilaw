What To Do Right Now If You've Been Charged

1

Exercise Your Right to Remain Silent

Do not discuss your case with anyone — not friends, family, or law enforcement — until you have spoken with an attorney. Anything you say can and will be used against you.

2

Do Not Consent to Searches or Questioning

Politely but firmly decline any searches or police questioning without your criminal defense attorney present. This is your constitutional right.

3

Write Down Everything You Remember

Document what happened, who was present, what was said, and the sequence of events as accurately as possible while memory is fresh.

4

Save All Paperwork

Preserve every document — citations, court date notices, bond paperwork, police reports, and any written communications related to your charges.

5

Call Rev. RJ Dieken, Esq. Before Your First Court Date

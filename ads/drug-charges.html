What We Defend

Types of Drug Charges in Montana

Montana drug laws carry serious consequences at every level. Here's what each charge means — and how we approach your defense.

Simple Possession

Possessing a controlled substance for personal use. Penalties depend on the drug schedule and amount — ranging from misdemeanor to felony. First-time offenders may have diversion or treatment options.

Defense: Unlawful search & seizure

Possession with Intent

Prosecutors use circumstantial evidence — scales, packaging, large amounts of cash, or quantity — to upgrade possession to intent to distribute. This carries much harsher penalties than simple possession.

Defense: Challenging intent inference

Distribution & Delivery

Selling, transferring, or delivering a controlled substance is a felony in Montana. Charges near a school or involving a minor dramatically increase penalties. We challenge the chain of evidence at every link.

Defense: Entrapment, informant credibility

Drug Trafficking

Montana's most serious drug offense. Trafficking is triggered by quantity thresholds and carries mandatory minimum sentences and potential federal prosecution. The stakes have never been higher.

Felony — mandatory minimums apply

Paraphernalia

Possession of drug paraphernalia — pipes, syringes, scales, or other equipment — is a separate criminal charge in Montana, often stacked with possession charges. We work to consolidate or dismiss stacked charges.

Defense: Ownership & purpose disputes

Federal Drug Charges

When drug activity crosses state lines or involves large quantities, the DEA or FBI may prosecute federally. Federal sentencing guidelines are far harsher. We defend in both Montana state and federal district court.

Federal court — no parole, strict minimums

Your Defense

How We Fight Drug Charges

A drug charge is not a conviction. The Constitution provides powerful protections — and we exploit every procedural weakness in the state's case.

Unlawful Search & Seizure

The Fourth Amendment prohibits unreasonable searches. If police searched your car, home, or person without a valid warrant or lawful exception, all evidence discovered may be suppressed — gutting the prosecution's case.

Chain of Custody Breaks

Drug evidence must be meticulously tracked from seizure to lab analysis to courtroom. Any gap or mishandling in the chain of custody can render evidence unreliable and inadmissible.

Lack of Possession / Knowledge

The state must prove you knowingly possessed the substance. If drugs were found in a shared space, a vehicle with multiple occupants, or belonged to someone else, we attack the element of knowing possession directly.

Entrapment

If law enforcement induced, persuaded, or coerced you into committing a drug offense you wouldn't otherwise have committed, the entrapment defense may apply — and can result in a full dismissal of charges.

Lab Analysis Challenges

Crime lab results are not infallible. We scrutinize lab protocols, analyst qualifications, contamination risks, and testing methods. Improper analysis can invalidate the identification of the alleged substance entirely.

Plea Reduction & Diversion

Not every case goes to trial. For eligible clients — especially first-time offenders — we negotiate aggressively for charge reductions, deferred sentences, treatment court, or diversion programs that can keep a conviction off your record.

What Happens After a Drug Arrest

1

Arrest & Search

Police may arrest you following a traffic stop, surveillance operation, search warrant, or tip from an informant. How the search was conducted is often the most important fact in your case.

Say nothing — invoke your right to counsel immediately
2

Booking & Bail

You'll be processed, fingerprinted, and held pending bail. For felony drug charges, bail can be substantial. We can appear at bail hearings to argue for release or reduced bail conditions so you can prepare your defense from home.

Call us — we appear at bail hearings
3

Arraignment

Formal charges are read and you enter a plea. This is not the time to plead guilty. With an attorney present, we enter a not-guilty plea, preserve your options, and begin building a defense strategy.

Never attend arraignment without counsel
4

Evidence Discovery & Investigation

We obtain all police reports, lab results, warrant applications, and surveillance records. We look for constitutional violations, procedural errors, and inconsistencies that create reasonable doubt or grounds for suppression.

Thorough investigation changes outcomes
5

Pretrial Motions & Negotiation

We file suppression motions to exclude illegally obtained evidence. We negotiate with prosecutors for reduced charges, deferred prosecution, or treatment alternatives. Many cases resolve favorably here — without a trial.

This phase determines most outcomes
6

Trial or Resolution

If a favorable plea isn't reached, we take your case to trial. We cross-examine officers and lab analysts, challenge every piece of evidence, and present a compelling defense to a jury of your peers. We go in ready to win.
